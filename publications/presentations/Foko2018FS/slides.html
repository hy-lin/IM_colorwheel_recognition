Mixture model

  • Two parameters:
    • \(\kappa\): precision of recall
    • \(P_m\): probability of recalling the target

Bayesian inference rule

  • To adapt the recall model to recognition, we used the Bayesian inference rule.

\[ d(x, \varphi) = \log[\frac{p("change"|x, \varphi)}{p("same"|x, \varphi)}] \\ = \log[\frac{p(x, \varphi|"change")\cdot p("change")}{p(x, \varphi|"same")\cdot p("same")}]\]

  • You recall the color from the probed location \(x\) and, given the probe color \(\varphi\), evaluate the probability of change or same.

Bayesian inference rule

  • If the recalled color is similar enough to the probe color, respond same
  • Otherwise, respond change
  • How similar is determined by the assumed recall distribution.

Experiment

  • 20 participants
  • Four sessions, 512 trials per session
    • 1 session of full recall
    • 1 session of full recognition
    • 2 session of recall and recognition
  • Setsize: 1, 2, 4, 6.
